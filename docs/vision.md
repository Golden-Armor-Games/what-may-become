# What May Become - Game Design Vision

> A hero-based real-time strategy RPG where every choice carves your legend—or ends it.

## Core Philosophy

What May Become is built on the principle that **choices matter**. In a genre often filled with respawns, save-scumming, and consequence-free decisions, we're creating a world where your actions have weight. When you make a choice, doors close. When you die, you stay dead—unless someone or something intervenes.

## The Opening

Every player begins the same way: your town is under attack.

What happens next defines who you become:

- **Lead**: Rally your townspeople, take command, face the threat head-on
- **Cower**: Hide and hope it passes, survive but earn no glory
- **Run**: Flee to safety, live to fight another day with nothing
- **Defend**: Stand your ground alone, protect what you can

Each choice branches into different starting conditions, relationships, and reputation. There is no "right" answer—only your answer.

## Fame System

Recognition in What May Become flows through four tiers:

### Local Fame
- Known in your immediate area
- Villagers recognize you
- Local merchants give small discounts
- Minor quests become available

### City Fame
- Known throughout the region
- City guards acknowledge you
- Access to city-level politics and missions
- Can recruit from a wider pool

### Realm Fame
- Known across the entire realm
- Nobility takes notice
- Major political influence
- Commands respect (or fear) everywhere

### Godly Fame
- The Gods themselves know your name
- Divine intervention becomes possible
- World-changing quests unlock
- Your legend echoes through time

Fame can be positive or negative. A notorious villain achieves fame just as a beloved hero does.

## Permadeath

Death is permanent. When your hero falls, they're gone.

### Resurrection Paths

Death is not always the end—but cheating it requires extraordinary means:

- **Divine Intervention**: A God may choose to return you, but at what cost?
- **Powerful Magic**: Rare and costly resurrection spells exist
- **Higher-Level Players**: A powerful enough player might have the means to bring you back
- **Unfinished Business**: Some say those with enough willpower refuse to stay dead

The threat of true death creates tension in every encounter. Running away is a valid strategy. Discretion truly is the better part of valor.

## Skill Progression

Skills improve through use, not experience points.

Cast 100 fireballs? You're a novice.
Cast 1,000? You're competent.
Cast 10,000? Your fireballs are devastating.

This creates natural specialization. Players become known for what they actually do, not what they've allocated points toward.

## Army Management

Heroes can command forces, but it comes at a cost.

### Division of Power
- Split your army into units with separate commanders
- Sacrifice your own strength to empower devoted followers
- Loyal troops can operate independently

### Wounded System
Combat survivors who are wounded don't simply disappear:
- They can teach skills to others
- They can work in towns, improving production
- They can gather resources
- They can recover and fight again

No soldier is disposable. Every loss is felt.

## Strategic Holdings

### Towns
- Can be built, upgraded, and fortified
- Produce resources based on workers and improvements
- Require defense against attacks
- Form the economic backbone of your power

### Keeps
- **Limited number exist in the world**
- Provide significant resource bonuses
- Strategic control points
- Worth fighting (and dying) for

The scarcity of keeps creates natural conflict. There will never be enough for everyone.

## Communication & Information

Information is power, and it's deliberately limited.

### Town Criers
- Spread news between towns
- Can be bribed, killed, or manipulated
- Information travels slowly

### Message Birds
- Faster communication over distance
- Can be intercepted
- Limited capacity

### No Global Chat
- No instant worldwide communication
- Rumors spread organically
- Fear of the unknown is preserved

You won't always know what's happening across the realm. That uncertainty is intentional.

## Quest System

### Consequential Choices
Every major quest decision:
- Closes other paths permanently
- May upset opposing Gods or factions
- Affects your standing with various groups
- Shapes the world state

### Gods & Factions
- Multiple Gods with competing interests
- Helping one often angers another
- Faction allegiances matter
- Neutrality has its own consequences

## Open-Source Storytelling

The community shapes the world.

### Story Pipeline
1. Community members submit quest/story proposals
2. Proposals go through review process
3. Approved content enters the game
4. Authors credited in-game

### Guidelines
- Must fit the world's tone and lore
- Must present meaningful choices
- Must have consequences
- Must be implementable

## The Three Realms

Inspired by the distinct identity of DAoC's three-realm design — each realm should feel like a completely different world.

### The North (Working Title: Greyhelm)
- **Feel:** Cold, bleak, survival-focused. Viking/Norse mythology grounding.
- **Palette:** Slate grays, ice blues, muted browns, frozen earth tones
- **Environment:** Tundra, sparse pine forests, frozen coastlines, stone keeps
- **People:** Stoic, clan-focused, deeply superstitious of the old gods
- **Strength:** Warriors and berserkers, brutal melee dominance
- **Weakness:** Limited magic, harsh resource scarcity

### The Middle Realm (Working Title: Valenmoor)
- **Feel:** Arthurian/medieval grounded. Neither utopia nor ruin. The contested center.
- **Palette:** Warm earth tones, golden fields, stone grays, autumn colors
- **Environment:** Rolling plains, farmland, marshes, walled cities, the classic RPG heartland
- **People:** Divided — knights and commoners, nobility and serfs, faith and politics
- **Strength:** Balanced — cavalry, faith magic, siege warfare
- **Weakness:** Internal political fracture, slow to unite

### The South/Forest Realm (Working Title: Thornveil)
- **Feel:** Celtic/Irish mythology grounding. Ancient, mysterious, deeply forested.
- **Palette:** Deep greens, mossy browns, bioluminescent accents at night, dappled light
- **Environment:** Ancient old-growth forest, hidden groves, vine-covered ruins, fog-shrouded paths
- **People:** Druids, rangers, fae-touched races. They don't conquer — they endure.
- **Strength:** Nature magic, guerrilla warfare, animal companions
- **Weakness:** No fixed fortifications, hard to hold territory

### Realm-vs-Realm (RvR)
The contested frontier between realms — keeps, border towns, chokepoints. This is where reputations are made or ended. The limited number of keeps exist here, and holding them across realm lines is the highest form of achievement.

## Technical Vision

### Browser-First
- WASM-based client for browser play
- No downloads required for basic play
- Accessible on modest hardware
- Native client available for enhanced performance

### Real-Time Multiplayer
- SpacetimeDB for seamless real-time state
- Server-authoritative for fairness
- Low-latency updates
- Persistent world state

## Target Experience

What May Become is for players who want:
- Meaningful decisions with real consequences
- A world that doesn't hold their hand
- Emergent stories from player interaction
- The thrill of genuine risk
- A game that respects their time and choices

It is not for players who want:
- Casual drop-in/drop-out gameplay
- Grinding without consequence
- Power fantasies without risk
- Story that happens regardless of choices
